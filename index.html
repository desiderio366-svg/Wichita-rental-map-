About the semiconductor markers: Integra Technologies (acquired by Micross in early 2025) operates a real, currently-staffed test/assembly site on N. Rock Road in NE Wichita today. Separately, a much larger ~$1.8–2.9B "mega-fab" was proposed for Bel Aire (Rock Rd & K-254) — but as of the most recent public reporting that project is in limbo: Kansas's original $304M+ incentive package expired in October 2024, federal CHIPS funding has been offered but not finalized, and Micross hasn't confirmed it will proceed at the original scale. Treat the Bel Aire pin as a speculative catalyst, not a sure thing.
About the aviation/defense markers: Wichita's "Air Capital of the World" manufacturing base is arguably a more reliable job-demand catalyst than the chip mega-fab. Most notably, Boeing officially reacquired and re-branded the former Spirit AeroSystems Wichita campus in December 2025 after Boeing's acquisition of Spirit AeroSystems closed — a confirmed, currently-operating large employer, not a proposal. Textron Aviation (Cessna/Beechcraft) and McConnell Air Force Base round out a deep, decades-old manufacturing/defense employment base that supports rental demand across south and southwest Wichita independent of the semiconductor story.
About rent, schools & amenities: Average-rent figures are modeled estimates blending RentCafe/Apartments.com neighborhood rent data with each area's price tier — some (Delano, Historic Midtown, North Central, Fairmount, Southwest Wichita) match published neighborhood-level figures directly; others are reasonable estimates since named micro-neighborhoods here don't always align with apartment-data boundaries. School notes use GreatSchools-style 1–10 ratings where a specific school could be confirmed (e.g., College Hill Elementary 5/10, Wheatland Elementary/Andover 8/10) and district-reputation ranges elsewhere — always verify the exact attendance zone for a specific address at GreatSchools.org or USD 259's Find Your School tool, since zones can be narrower than a named neighborhood. Amenities are illustrative highlights, not exhaustive lists.
Read before you buy: Crime and livability figures are pulled from third-party aggregators (CrimeGrade, NeighborhoodScout, AreaVibes) and real-estate blogs, not raw police records. Several named "micro-neighborhoods" have only a few hundred to ~2,000 residents, so a handful of incidents can swing their per-100k crime rate wildly. Pin locations and the boundary outlines drawn on click are approximate, illustrative centroids/extents — not official parcel, ward, or neighborhood-association boundaries. Verify exact boundaries, current crime activity, and zoning via wichita.gov/Crime-Stats, the city's ArcGIS crime layer, and Sedgwick County GIS before making any purchase. This is not financial or legal advice.

How to use it

Tap a marker to see crime, rent, schools, amenities, and investor notes, and to draw its approximate boundary. Toggle layers above to isolate a tier, or switch color mode to see rent level instead. Tap a row in the rent ranking list to jump straight to that pin. On phones, tap ☰ to open this panel.
